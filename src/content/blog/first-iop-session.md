If you have just scheduled your first Intensive Outpatient Program (IOP) session, you probably have questions. Will I have to talk in front of strangers? Will they make me share things I do not want to share? What if I cannot keep up?

These are normal questions. Here is what actually happens.

## Before you arrive

Our intake team calls you 24 to 48 hours before your first session to confirm the time, the building entrance, and anything you need to bring. If you take medication, bring the bottles or a current list — your clinician will ask. If you have insurance cards or a referral, bring those. Otherwise, just bring yourself.

Parking is on-site. The front door opens 15 minutes before the session starts. Our intake coordinator greets you at the desk, walks you to the group room, and introduces you to the facilitator.

## The first 15 minutes

You start in a small orientation circle — usually 6 to 10 people, plus two facilitators. Nobody is forced to share on day one. Most groups open with a brief check-in ("name, one word for how you got here"), and that is it. Your facilitator will explain the ground rules: what stays in the room, what does not, and how to ask for a break.

## The middle 60 minutes

The bulk of the session is structured around a topic — triggers, coping skills, the stages of change, relapse prevention. You are welcome to listen. You are welcome to participate at whatever level feels safe. The facilitators are trained to handle silence; they will not call on you.

## The closing 15 minutes

We close with a check-out. Each person shares one word for how they are feeling leaving the room. Your facilitator gives you the date of the next session and the topic. If you need a one-on-one follow-up before then, you can ask for it at the desk.

## What you do not have to do

You do not have to share your story on day one. You do not have to explain what brought you here. You do not have to commit to anything beyond showing up for the next session. If IOP is not the right level of care for you, your clinician will tell you, and we will help you find what is.
